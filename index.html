Our Investment Strategies

Long/Short Equity

Our flagship strategy combines deep fundamental research with quantitative screening to identify mispriced securities across global markets. We maintain a disciplined approach to both long and short positions, focusing on companies with strong valuation metrics and identifiable catalysts.

Quantitative Value

Leveraging proprietary algorithms and machine learning models, we systematically identify undervalued securities with strong balance sheets and improving fundamentals. Our quantitative approach allows us to efficiently scan thousands of securities across multiple markets.

Event-Driven

We capitalize on corporate events such as mergers, spin-offs, restructurings, and other special situations where pricing inefficiencies often emerge. Our team's experience in corporate finance and legal analysis gives us an edge in assessing event outcomes.

Sustainable Growth

Focusing on companies driving innovation in renewable energy, clean technology, and sustainable infrastructure. We combine traditional financial analysis with ESG metrics to identify leaders in the transition to a low-carbon economy.

About Ingram Capital

Founded with the vision of delivering superior risk-adjusted returns through disciplined investment strategies, Ingram Capital has grown into a respected investment management firm serving institutional and high-net-worth investors.

Our team combines decades of experience across hedge funds, investment banks, and asset management firms. We bring together fundamental research, quantitative analysis, and rigorous risk management to identify investment opportunities others may overlook.

We operate with a performance-driven culture that emphasizes transparency, accountability, and alignment of interests with our investors. Our investment professionals are significant investors in our funds, ensuring our interests are fully aligned with those of our clients.

Our approach is rooted in deep fundamental analysis, complemented by proprietary quantitative models that help us identify valuation anomalies and emerging trends across global markets.
